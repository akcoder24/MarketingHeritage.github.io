"Memory of a widowed queen"
~Rani ki Vav





A place that redefines love. If taj mahal was built by a husband for a wife, Rani ki Vav displayed the love of a widowed queen to her dear husband. Deep in these stepwell lies not only the symbol of love but also one of the finest temples with the highlight as water. Constructed during the Chalukyas dynasty this temple has Steps leading down through multiple levels with lines of carved pillars and more than 800 sculptures, mostly on Vishnu-avatar themes, as well as striking geometric patterns.

Kanch Mahal

A beautiful exclusive ladies’ resort was built by emperor Akbar very close to his tomb in Sikandar. This exquisite monument is shaped in the form of a square and was originally encircled by a beautiful garden with regular watercourses, causeways and tanks alike. This beauty also served her purpose as emperor Jehangir’s royal hunting lodge. It is called Kanch Mahal because of the abundant use of tile work in its construction.

Undavalli Caves

With such rich heritage, these are bound to be 100s of years of buried civilization in our rocks which frequently emerge out as caves and become a significant door to the past. One of such unexplored historical places in India is the Undavalli Caves in the Guntur district of Andhra Pradesh. The Undavalli Caves are one of the best examples of Indian rock-cut architecture. The caves are best known as the 'four-story cave' and have a huge recreated statues of Vishnu.
